HARDWARE

Smart Energy Meter

  • Robust and Sleek design
  • Variant: Single phase & Three phase
  • Communication: Two way, Open protocol (DLMS), GSM/RF
  • Internal relay for Remote Load disconnect/ Reconnect
  • Anti tamper and fraud detection
  • Security - Password protected user login and parameter settings
  • Online data downloading through GSM/GPRS communication.

Group Smart Energy Meter

  • Compact Meters connected to a local data concentrator unit are mounted in a distribution board outside the consumer premises.
    • Single Phase kWh meter
    • Measures & Display kWh
    • Optional Parameters :I,V &kW
  • RF based Home Display Unit/Consumer Interface Unit(optional).
  • HES collects all the meters data from Main DCU.
    • Local Data Concentrator Unit(LDCU) with RF communication
    • Transfers metering information from meter to Home Display Units.
    • Transfers Metering information of a group of Meter to Main DCU.

SOFTWARE

Admin Power Management Platform

  • Remote meter reading - It saves the cost of a human meter reader and the resulting mistakes and it also allows more measurements, and remote provisioning.
  • Provide remote access for switching off or on the power supply of its users. Thereby, providing a better mechanism to check for bill-defaulting users.
  • Identify anomalies and thefts of electricity by analyzing the consumption data of its users.
  • Providing real-time data useful for balancing electric loads and reducing power outages (black- outs). Thereby, also saving electricity wastage by surplus production.
  • Enabling dynamic pricing or Time-of-use Tariffs (raising or lowering the cost of electricity based on demand).

Group Smart Energy Meter

  • Consumers can be informed remotely (historical data) or locally (real-time data) on energy costs. Also, they can reduce costs by increasing energy consumption during off-peak cheaper tariff periods.
  • Notifications can be sent to users on this application whenever the admin(s) find the possibility of anomalies or thefts by analyzing the power consumption.
  • Machine learning algorithms (For example : ARIMA) can be leveraged to estimate the power consumption of users on a monthly/yearly basis.
